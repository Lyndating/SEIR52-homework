John Von Neumann

If Turing was the father of computing then John Von Neumann was the cool rich uncle!
Was a consultant for the Manhattan project. And employed by the military and navy. Worked on a project with the ENIAC computer, a vacuum tube computer running on decimal arithmitic that was supposed to be programmed to shoot guns. He tried to make it into a general purpose computer and used it to do some calculations relating to the development of the atomic bomb.
Pioneered creating general purpose, electronic computers, that were binary based. There were several "Turing Complete" computers at the time but they were either general purpose but electro-mechanical or electronic and special purpose. At Moore School at Pennsylvania, worked on EDVAC(electronic, discrete, variable, automatic, computer), the successor to ENIAC in 1946.
Famous for creating Von Neumann Architecture. Basically means: to build a computer you need a store (memory) to hold instructions an data. A control unit (CPU) and an arithmitic and logic unit (now also in the CPU) and input units. Still very similar to computers today.
